The Grant

$2,500 for autonomous weed detection

I received a $2,500 grant from Emergent Ventures for my autonomous weed detection drone - a system that identifies weeds in agricultural fields and enables targeted pesticide application, reducing waste and improving crop yields.

The grant went toward further development and testing of the drone system. Alongside the funding, I was invited to the 2024 EV UnConference in Washington, D.C. - a gathering of fellows across every field imaginable.

02

The UnConference

A different kind of gathering

The EV UnConference in Washington D.C. is unlike any traditional conference. There is no pre-set agenda, no passive audience. The content is generated entirely by participants - everyone proposes topics, leads discussions, and contributes in a meaningful way.

It's choose-your-own-adventure. At any moment multiple sessions are running in parallel, and participants are encouraged to move between them freely. The schedule starts blank and fills in at the start of the day - then evolves as conversations merge and shift.

Participant-Driven

Unlike traditional conferences with pre-set agendas, every attendee proposes topics and leads discussions. The agenda is built on the day.

Move Freely

Multiple sessions run in parallel at all times. Participants are actively encouraged to move between them - there are no fixed seats.

The Board

A large grid representing the schedule sits at the centre of the event. Time slots start blank and fill collaboratively at the start of each day.

About EV
